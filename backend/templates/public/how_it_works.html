How it works

The real technical process — no black box

Sentinel works after Google delivers your mail, not before. It is not a pre-delivery gateway or an SMTP filter — it analyses and acts on messages that have already reached Gmail.

1

Connect Gmail

An administrator starts the connection from Connected Mailboxes.

2

Complete Google OAuth

Google's consent screen grants Sentinel the specific scopes it needs — never your password.

3

Activate protection

Sentinel verifies its Gmail labels exist and begins monitoring.

4

Detect mailbox changes

Gmail API polling (with optional Pub/Sub push) detects new messages.

5

Retrieve the full email

The complete message is fetched — not just a preview or snippet.

6

Parse MIME content

Headers, body, and attachment structure are extracted safely.

7

Analyse sender & authentication

Display name, Reply-To, Return-Path, and SPF/DKIM/DMARC results are checked.

8

Analyse links & attachments

Real link destinations and attachment metadata are inspected.

9

Run the classifier

A trained machine-learning model produces a phishing probability and prediction confidence.

10

Generate the operational verdict

Model output and structured findings combine into Legitimate, Needs Review, or Phishing.

11

Apply the Gmail action

A label is applied; Phishing is quarantined via a label move — nothing is ever deleted.

12

Investigate & respond

An administrator reviews the incident and confirms, releases, or escalates it.

Sentinel does not intercept mail before delivery. Detection begins after Gmail has already accepted and stored the message.
